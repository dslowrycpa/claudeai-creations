01 The Meet Has Changed Shape

A 46-day, 10-week meet — not the traditional 40

Before tuning anything else, the engine has to know this is a structurally different meet than 2023–2025. The footprint is bigger, the calendar is irregular, and Belmont's closure has pulled premier races and the entire spring prep circuit onto unfamiliar ground.

The 2026 calendar (verified)

  • Opens Friday, July 3; closes Labor Day, Monday, Sept 7.
  • 46 racing days across 10 weeks — an expansion from the traditional 40-day meet.
  • Dark Mondays & Tuesdays as usual, plus dark Wednesdays through July 26.
  • Four-day weeks (Thu–Sun) July 9–26; five-day weeks resume Wed July 29.
  • Six-day closing stretch, Sept 2 – Labor Day.

Source: NYRA 2026 schedule announcement & official Saratoga calendar.

Why it's bigger this year

Belmont Park is closed for reconstruction. As a one-time event, the July 4th Racing Festival moved to Saratoga, bringing Belmont-signature races onto the Spa card: the G1 Belmont Derby ($750K), G1 Belmont Oaks ($600K), and G2 Suburban all run July 4. NYRA has stated the meet reverts to the traditional 40-day schedule in 2027 when Belmont reopens.

Source: NYRA. Belmont Park reopens for live racing September 2026.

03 Where the Horses Come From

Shippers & origin-track form

You asked specifically how Churchill, Gulfstream and Aqueduct horses perform. Here is the honest state of the data — and a structural change in 2026 that bears directly on the engine's “last-race-track” logic.

Honest data limitation — read this first

Clean, public, aggregate win-rate-by-origin-track tables for Saratoga (e.g. “horses last out at Gulfstream win X% at the Spa”) are not published by Equibase, NYRA, DRF or any free source as a downloadable stat. Anyone presenting a single tidy “Gulfstream shippers hit 19%” number is either citing a paywalled trip/pace product or fabricating it. The right move is to have TrackSmart compute these from the past performances it already ingests — the data is in the PPs (last-raced track is a field on every line). Section 08 specs that build. Everything below is what is verifiably knowable now.

The 2026 structural shift that changes “last race track”

This is the single most important shipper finding in the report, because it breaks naive last-track matching:

Belmont is closed → the NY feeder moved

With Belmont Park under reconstruction, NYRA ran its 2026 spring NY-circuit racing at Aqueduct under the “Belmont at the Big A” banner. So the bulk of horses arriving at Saratoga 2026 off a New York prep last raced at Aqueduct — but the chart/PP track label may read as a Belmont-branded meet at the Aqueduct strip.

Why it matters for the model

Aqueduct's main track is a smaller, tighter oval than Belmont's. Recent NY route form in 2026 was earned around Aqueduct's configuration, not Belmont's sweeping mile-and-a-half. Engine must (a) map the “Belmont at the Big A” label to the Aqueduct node, and (b) avoid crediting horses with Belmont-style two-turn experience they didn't actually get.

Source: NYRA (Aqueduct closing weekend / Belmont reopening Sept 2026; “Belmont at Aqueduct” spring race references in 2026 entries).

The one verified shipper datapoint

Published 2025 analysis credited Todd Pletcher with a ~21% win rate specifically with shippers at the meet — consistent with his overall 24.4% and a reminder that elite barns ship to win, not to school. Treat “first start at the meet” as barn-dependent, not a blanket negative.

The structural route trap (verified, high-value)

Saratoga's dirt-route geometry is unusual and trips up shippers every year: the Spa runs no one-turn miles and no 1‑1/16-mile dirt routes — its dirt routes are two-turn at 1‑1/8 miles or longer. Belmont historically ran most of its dirt routes (1m, 1‑1/16, 1‑1/8) around one turn. So a horse with sharp “route” form earned around one turn elsewhere is facing a genuinely different test here. Prefer horses with proven two-turn form when handicapping Saratoga dirt routes.

04 The Surface Itself

Track bias: dirt sprints reward forward position

A recurring, documented Saratoga dirt-sprint profile — useful as a prior, with the standing caveat that within-meet bias swings day-to-day with weather and rail placement.

6-furlong dirt sprints

~33% won by front-runners, ~31% by early-pace types — roughly 64% of these races go to horses on or near the lead.

7-furlong dirt sprints

~43% front-runners, ~26% early-pace — about 69% won with tactical speed and forward placement.

Source: published Saratoga dirt track-profile data (Today's Racing Digest). Treat as a baseline prior, not a daily certainty — the engine should still update bias from each day's results.

Engine implication

Pace-shape modeling should carry a positive early-position coefficient on dirt sprints, strongest at 6–7f, and discount deep closers in those splits unless the projected pace is genuinely contested. Pair this with the two-turn route rule above for a coherent dirt model.

How to actually use this at the Spa

Freshman-sire rank alone is a weak signal; it's strongest combined with barn and price point. The high-value intersection: a Pletcher/Brown/Ward first-time starter by a speed-precocious value sire (Drain the Clock, Golden Pal, Nashville) in a 6f dirt or 5.5f turf juvenile sprint — that stacks the trainer 2yo edge (Section 02), the dirt-sprint speed bias (Section 04), and a sire bred to fire early. Discount marquee-fee, distance-leaning sires (Flightline) in short first-out sprints.

06 The Established Young Sires

Sophomore (2nd-crop) & third-crop sires

For 3yo and lightly-raced 4yo fields, the sophomore and third-crop lists carry more signal than the freshman list because the sample is real. Verified anchors below; the engine should refresh full standings live, as these rankings move with every graded result.

Sophomore sires — oldest are 3yos in 2026

This group is the 2025 freshman class, one year on. Verified anchor: Yaupon (son of the late Uncle Mo; Spendthrift) was the No. 1 first-crop sire of 2025 — dominating juvenile categories and leading by a wide earnings margin — so he heads this list entering 2026.

Bolt d'Oro (the 2022 champion freshman sire) also belongs here as an established young dirt sire, with multiple black-type 3yos reported in 2025.

Third-crop sires — oldest are 4yos in 2026

This group is the 2024 freshman class. Verified anchor: Vekoma was the Champion First-Crop Sire of 2024 and the No. 1 Second-Crop Sire of 2025, with his fee climbing to $100K — a clear, sustained leader for this crop year.

Why this section is deliberately conservative

U.S. second- and third-crop standings for 2026 are not cleanly scrapeable from free sources right now (BloodHorse's sire-list tables are bot-walled; much of the freely indexed crop data is European). Rather than quote stale or mis-attributed U.S. numbers, the verified anchors above are stated and the rest is flagged as a live-pull task (Section 08). This is the “must not assume” discipline you asked for.

08 Putting It in the Model

Recommended TrackSmart AI engine revisions

Concrete, prioritized changes derived only from the verified findings above. Items marked “compute” are builds against the PPs the engine already ingests — not external assumptions.

  1. Rescale meet-volume thresholds for 46 days. Any logic keyed to raw win counts (“leading trainer pace,” hot-barn flags) needs roughly a +15% adjustment vs. 2023–2025 40-day baselines, or convert all volume metrics to per-start rates to stay meet-length-agnostic.
  2. Map the “Belmont at the Big A” label to the Aqueduct node. Treat 2026 NY-circuit prep form as Aqueduct-configuration form. Do not credit Belmont-style sweeping two-turn experience that horses didn't actually run. last_track in {Belmont@BigA, Aqueduct} → NY_circuit_node.
  3. Build the origin-track shipper module (compute). From PP last-raced-track fields, compute rolling Saratoga win/ITM rates by origin for Churchill, Gulfstream, Aqueduct, Woodbine, Monmouth, Kentucky Downs/Ellis. Replace any hard-coded shipper assumption with these computed priors. This is the proper home for the question “how do Gulfstream/Churchill horses do.”
  4. Encode the two-turn dirt-route filter. Saratoga has no one-turn miles / no 1‑1/16 dirt routes; dirt routes are two-turn at 1‑1/8m+. Add a two-turn-proven feature and penalize horses whose route form is one-turn-only.
  5. Add the dirt-sprint early-position coefficient. Positive weight for front-runners / early-pace types at 6f (~64% on-pace) and 7f (~69% on-pace), conditioned on projected pace contention. Keep a daily-bias updater on top of this prior.
  6. Weight 2yo barns and sire×barn×distance stacks. Boost Pletcher, Brown, Ward first-time juveniles; intersect with speed-precocious value freshman sires (Drain the Clock, Golden Pal, Nashville, Life Is Good) in short sprints. Discount marquee distance-leaning sires (Flightline) first-out short.
  7. Model rider value as payoff-adjusted, not win-count. Flag Dylan Davis-type positive-ROI riders; treat Jose Ortiz as an efficiency edge when the public overbets Irad. Carry per-meet favorite-mutuel context.
  8. Hard-code annual NY-bred coefficients. Sires: Central Banker, Solomini, Honest Mischief, King for a Day up; Laoban decaying. Breeders: Broman, McMahon, Sequel, Stonewall, Windylea as a quality tier. Refresh from the NY Fund leaderboard each January.
  9. Compute the NY-bred damsire distribution (compute). Don't import a national broodmare-sire list; derive it from state-bred PPs to capture the actual NY population.
  10. Refresh live sire standings weekly during the meet. Freshman and sophomore lists reshuffle at the Spa — schedule a weekly pull so juvenile-race weights track the current leaderboard, not the June snapshot.

One caution worth stating plainly

The single biggest risk to the 2026 model is treating it like a normal 40-day Belmont-fed meet. It isn't: it's longer, the calendar is lumpy, the premier July races are Belmont imports, and the entire NY prep circuit ran at Aqueduct under a Belmont brand. Items 01–04 in Section 08 exist specifically to keep the engine from making that assumption.

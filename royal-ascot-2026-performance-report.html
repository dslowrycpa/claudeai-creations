Standout Performances

The best calls — and the worst

Best of the Meet

Day 3 — Gold Cup (11 horses)
Predicted top 3 landed in exact 1-2-3 order: Scandinavia, Trawlerman, Sweet William. The cleanest call of the festival.
Day 4 — King Edward VII (6 horses)
All four top picks finished as the actual top four, just reshuffled — and Causeway nailed the win outright on top of it.
Day 5 — Queen Alexandra (13 horses)
Illinois won exactly as predicted, with French Master right behind him in the exact 2nd slot — a double exact-position hit.
Day 1 — King Charles III (26 horses)
An exact win in the largest field of opening day, when big fields were still the model's weak spot.

Worst of the Meet

Day 3 — Britannia & Buckingham Palace (33 & 32 horses)
Back-to-back total wipeouts. Every actual finisher in both races came from horses the engine had flatly rated "Toss."
Day 2 — Royal Hunt Cup, Kensington Palace, Windsor Castle
Three straight complete misses to close Day 2, all in fields of 25 runners or more.
Day 5 — Jersey Stakes (17 horses)
The most surprising miss of the meet — a modest 17-horse field, yet zero overlap between the top 4 and the actual top 4.
